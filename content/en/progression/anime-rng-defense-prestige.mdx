<Section icon={Swords} title="Elite Team Building and Unit Placement">

Building a "Prestige" level team requires more than just high-rarity units; it requires tactical synergy. A balanced lineup must account for group clearing, single-target boss damage, and early-wave control. In **anime rng defense prestige**, the placement of your units on the map is just as critical as the units themselves.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Early Control">
    Place a dependable unit near the enemy spawn or at the first major curve. This prevents leakages while you save resources for your main carries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Deploy Core Carries">
    Identify the "Long-Path" sections of the map where enemies remain in range the longest. Place your S-Tier and A-Tier units here to maximize their total damage output per wave.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Prioritize Upgrades">
    Focus your in-battle currency on upgrading your strongest unit to its maximum level before moving to the next. One maxed-out carry is often better than three low-level defenders.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Monitor Unit Synergy">
    Ensure your team has a mix of attack types. Some units excel at deleting large groups (AOE), while others are designed to melt high-health targets.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Unit Role | Best Placement | Key Attribute |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Main Carry** | Winding paths, corners | High AOE or DPS scaling |
| **Support/Utility** | Centralized locations | Buffs or crowd control |
| **Early Defender** | Near enemy entrance | Fast attack speed, low cost |
| **Boss Killer** | End of long straights | High single-target damage |

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Success">
Units that feature "Wave Clear" capabilities should always be your first priority for upgrades. Clearing the bulk of enemies early in the path gives your single-target units more time to focus on tougher elite mobs.
</Callout>

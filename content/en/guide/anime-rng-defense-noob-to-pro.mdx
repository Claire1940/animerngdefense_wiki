<Section icon={Shield} title="Map Progression and Enemy Shield Mechanics">

As you advance through different maps (such as the starter world and the Naruto-themed world), the layout becomes larger, paths wind more, and enemies develop complex defensive mechanics. The most notable mechanic you will encounter is the **Enemy Shield**.

Shielded enemies cannot be damaged effectively by slow, single-hit heavy hitters. If you try to fight a shielded boss using only slow, high-damage towers, the shield will regenerate, and the boss will walk straight into your base. To counter this, you must build a balanced lineup that utilizes both multi-hit units and high-damage finishers.

| Unit Type | Attack Style | Main Role | Ideal Placement |
|--- |--- |--- |--- |
| **Multi-Hit (e.g., Kilo)** | Fast, low-damage rapid strikes | Shredding enemy shields | Directly at the enemy spawn exits |
| **AoE Damage (e.g., Fering)** | Slow, wide-area explosions | Clearing crowded enemy groups | On sharp, winding path corners |
| **Single-Target DPS (e.g., Eizen)** | Ultra-high single-target damage | Executing unshielded bosses | Along long straightaways near the middle |

<Callout type="tip" title="Placement Strategy">
Always place your fast-attacking multi-hit units at the very front of the enemy path. This ensures that by the time the enemy wave reaches your heavy-hitting AoE and single-target units, their shields are already broken, allowing your main damage dealers to deal full damage.
</Callout>

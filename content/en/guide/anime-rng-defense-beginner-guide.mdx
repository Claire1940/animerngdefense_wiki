<Section icon={Swords} title="Team Building and Placement Strategies">

A successful team build requires more than just equipping your rarest units. You need a balanced composition that can handle different phases of an enemy wave. Your team should consist of early-wave defenders, single-target boss killers, and area-of-effect (AoE) wave clearers. 

| Unit Role | Placement Priority | Upgrade Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early Control** | Near the enemy spawn point | Low (keep cheap to allow saving) |
| **Main Carry (DPS)** | Long straight paths or loops | High (max out first for boss waves) |
| **AoE Wave Clear** | U-turns and intersections | Medium (upgrade when swarms appear) |
| **Support / Slow** | Chokepoints | Low (just need the utility effect) |

When placing units, always look for spots where their range circle covers the maximum amount of the track. Placing a unit on an outer corner is generally less effective than placing it on the inside of a U-turn, where it can attack enemies as they approach, turn, and walk away.

<Callout type="tip" title="Placement Strategy">
Avoid spreading your upgrades evenly across all units. It is almost always better to have one fully upgraded carry unit than five low-level units. Maximize your carry first to clean up tough enemies easily.
</Callout>

</Section>
